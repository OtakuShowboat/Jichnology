Kouphichnium Nopcsa, 1923


DESCRIPTION: Parallel tracks that have an alternating set of impressions: 1. V-shaped or chevron shaped impressions; 2. Digitate impressions with several digits extending from a small impression subperpendicular to the long axis of the trackway. Heteropodous tracks with two primary forms: 1.) 2 Chevron-like series of 4 oval to round holes; 2.) Bifid V-shaped flabellar to birdfoot-like imprints with/without median drag marks (Hasiotis, 2002).

BEHAVIOR(S): The trace represents locomotion, reproductive, and feeding behavior. Complete body impressions represent resting or hiding behavior.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Marine to freshwater aquatic.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Marine and freshwater horseshoe crabs.

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Ordovician–recent for marine horseshoe crabs, Permian–Jurassic for freshwater horseshoe crabs.

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: (Hasiotis, 2002)

REMARKS: Some Nanopus reidiae (Haubold et al., 2005) may actually be Kouphichnium.
