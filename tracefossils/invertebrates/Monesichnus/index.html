Monesichnus (Roselli, 1987)


DESCRIPTION: Fusiform to ovate structures composed of an external, unpatterned, lumpy surface sometimes exhibiting a longitudinal groove and scratches. Galleries lead to an internal cavity or cells.

BEHAVIOR(S): Possible reproduction of dung or similar beetles.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Constructed in soils in terrestrial environments including alluvial and marginal lacustrine in arid to wet climates. Commonly found in the A and upper-part of the B horizons of soils and reflect the upper vadose zone of soil moisture.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Dung beetles.

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Eocene-recent.

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: (Emended: Genise and Laza, 1998)
