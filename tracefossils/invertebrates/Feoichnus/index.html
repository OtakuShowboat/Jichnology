Feoichnus Bellosi et al., 2001


DESCRIPTION: Dish-like, concave structures, hemispherical and concave upwards, walled and passively filled.

BEHAVIOR(S): Reproduction.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Occur in a wide variety of continental environments, most often found in well-drained soils.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Dung beetles

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Jurassic-recent

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: Emended by Bellosi, 2004. Hasiotis, 2006

REMARKS: 3D image of Feoichnus can be seen here. Often found in Coprinaesphera samples. Interpreted by Krause et al., 2008 as feeding traces by cicadas, however, research by Hasiotis (2002) and other dung beetle references show this trace to have been created by dung beetle larvae feeding on dung sausages.
