Macaronichnus Clifton and Thompson, 1978


DESCRIPTION: Densely packed, thin sub-vertical to sub-horizontal cylindrical smooth-sided burrows/shafts with circular cross section. Diameter is range from 2 to 5 mm. Longitudinally sinuous, never branch, but interpenetrations are common. May be meniscate.

BEHAVIOR(S): Deposit feeder that largely sustains itself on bacteria and organic matter on the surface of sand grains, which are ingested during locomotion.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Shallow marine near-shore high-energy sandy environments; intertidal and shallow subtidal deposits.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Polychaete worms (Ophelia).

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Cambrian-recent.
