Rogerella Saint-Seine, 1951


DESCRIPTION: Very deep borings of barnacles. Commonly found in the shells of brachiopods, gastropods, and bivalves. Opening is commonly ovoid in shape, sometimes tear drop.

BEHAVIOR(S): Domichnia, Fodinichnia; Trace maker bores into host shell/media for dwelling purposes to filter feed.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Shallow marine settings near areas with reefal structures in higher energy water.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Arthropods (Barnacles)

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Cambrian-recent
