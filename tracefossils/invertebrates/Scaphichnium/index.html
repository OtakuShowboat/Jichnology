Scaphichnium Bown and Kraus, 1983


DESCRIPTION: Hook- to small J-slaped cells with a bulbous termination. Internally composed of alternating menisci.

BEHAVIOR(S): Reproduction. Designed to hold young hatched insects, as well as food for the young.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Fully continental. Constructed in soils developed in alluvial and marginal lacustrine environments that developed in arid to wet climates.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Dung Beetles.

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Permian-recent.

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: (Hasiotis et al., 1993)(Hasiotis, 2002)
