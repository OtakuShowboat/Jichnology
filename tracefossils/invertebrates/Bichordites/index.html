Bichordites Plaziat and Mahmoudi, 1965


DESCRIPTION: Straight to winding, horizontal, cylindrical, unbranched mensicate burrow with a heart- to cylindrical-shaped central tube.

BEHAVIOR(S): Deposit feeding.

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Fully marine - Most commonly found in shallow-marine deposits

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Irregular echinoids (sea urchins).

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Cretaceous-recent.

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: (Emended: Uchman, 1995)(Ghent and Henderson, 1966)

REMARKS: Full relief burrow distinct from Laminites (Uchman, 1995, 1998)
