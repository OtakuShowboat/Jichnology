Entradichnus Ekdale and Picard, 1985


DESCRIPTION: Long, unbranched, unlined, backfilled burrow, gently and irregularly sinuous, oriented parallel to bedding . Circular in cross-section uniform diameter throughout entire length.

BEHAVIOR(S): Locomotion

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Eolian

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Cranefly larvae

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Jurassic-recent

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: (Ekdale, et al., 2007)

REMARKS: Morphologically similar to Beaconites
