--- layout: traces title: Eatonichnus ---

Eatonichnus Bown et al., 1998


DESCRIPTION: Fusiform to ovate structures composed of highly ornamented, compressed whorls that spiral around a central cavity.

BEHAVIOR(S): Dwelling and reproduction

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Found in various continental environments. Indicate a moderately moist soil, above the water table.

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Wasps, beetles

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Paleocene-recent

ADDITIONAL REFERENCES: Genise et al., 2000; Hasiotis, 2006
