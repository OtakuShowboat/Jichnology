Corymbipes Sarjeant and Langston, 1994


JUNIOR SYNONYM(S): N/A

DESCRIPTION: Digitigrade to semidigitigrade manus tracks and plantigrade pes tracks, manus tracks have digits II–V and pes tracks have all five digits, all digits have hooves. Manus’ digits are moderately large; digits II and III are directed inward, IV is forward and V is outward. Pes’ hooves are smaller than the manus’; I, the smallest, is directed inward, II–IV are forward and V is outward. Pes’ tarsals and metatarsals can be distinguishable.

BEHAVIOR(S): Repichnia

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Continental; terrestrial

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Animals from the order Mesonychia. Possibly Harpagolestes since it is found in the near the same area and almost the same time.

GEOLOGIC RANGE: Upper Eocene
