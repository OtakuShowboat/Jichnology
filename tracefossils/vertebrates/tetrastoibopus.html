Tetrastoibopus Sarjeant and Langston, 1994


JUNIOR SYNONYM(S): N/A

DESCRIPTION: Plantigrade to semiplantigrade tracks with manus and pes prints having digits II–IV present along with a digital pad. Claw tip impressions can be present. Digits II–IV have a small pad and are located at the front while digit V is larger and is laterally off to the side.

BEHAVIOR(S): Repichnia

ENVIRONMENTAL SETTINGS: Continental; terrestrial

POSSIBLE TRACEMAKERS: Early mammals belonging in Carnivora. They were likely created by the dog like carnivore Miacis (Miacoidea), since they are the appropriate size and they are found in the same region and formation as the footprints. The feet of Miacis, however, remains undescribed.

GEOLOGIC RANGE: upper Eocene
